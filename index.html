Guilherme Maeda
Researcher in robotics and robot motor skill learning


Eventually, our society will depend on robots as general-purpose helpers that will assist us throughout our entire lives. This vision requires robots capable of learning skills by themselves to manipulate and interact with the world in a way that is useful for us. My work involves the investigation and design of learning control methods, and their implementation and validation using real robots.

Phase Portrait Movement Primitives (PPMP)

Animals plan complex motor actions not only fast but seemingly with little effort even on unseen tasks. This natural sense of time and coordination motivates us to approach robot control from a motor skill learning perspective to design fast and computationally light controllers that can be learned autonomously by the robot under mild modeling assumptions. To this end, we propose Phase Portrait Movement Primitives. We tested the method on a task comprising 20 degrees-of-freedom using a hydraulic upper body humanoid. Click here for details.

Reinforcement Learning of Motor Skills
using Policy Search and Human Corrective Advice

A human-in-the-loop approach where human feedback can come at any time during the execution of an exploration roll-out. The feedback can also come sporadically, where most of the roll-outs do not even to include human feedback. This human feedback is seamlessly incorporated into the policy update as an informed/biased exploration noise. The learning rate increases by factors of 4 to 40 times depending on the task. You can watch the video of a ball-in-cup where the policy starts from a blank state here, and find the details of method in the paper here.
